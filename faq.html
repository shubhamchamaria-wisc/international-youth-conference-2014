1) What is IYC?

The International Youth Conference is a one of a kind annual event hosted by the IBDP of Jamnabai Narsee School. It is modeled along the lines of the G8, G14, G20, GSO and the GMC and follows the Harvard MUN procedure. It is intended for youngsters between the ages of 15 and 18, seeking a platform for discussion on world issues that are plaguing countries today. As a participant you can expect to be a delegate or representative of a nation or a special-interest group such as China or Greenpeace respectively. It is different from MUNs because no committee exists. Rather, discussion takes place among the leaders of the G8, G14 and the G20 countries.

2) When is IYC?

4th and 5th October, 2014

3) Where is IYC?

Sea Princess Hotel, Juhu Tara Road, Mumbai.

4) What should I expect at IYC?

An intellectual ground for discussion under the supervision of an experienced chair. Those new to the MUN - ing circuit will be at ease. A forum for your voice to be heard and worthy opponents to prove your mettle are among some of the things you should expect during IYC.

5) This is my first conference/MUN. Am I up to the task?

The Organizing Committee has always been especially careful to make this event "newcomer-friendly". At the IYC, it does not matter whether you are a MUN- ing expert or a novice; every participant has something to gain.

6) How should I prepare for IYC?

Reading up on the various topics is a good start. Finding a key piece of information beforehand helps you prepare your argument well in advance, whereas finding a key piece of argument at the last minute may just impair your argument.

7) Can I register on arrival?

As much as we would appreciate you attending IYC, due to logistical constraints, it will not be possible for us to accommodate you, if you have not registered prior to the deadlines.

8) Where can I find resources about the topics?

Our topics at the IYC are presently global issues, and thus not too difficult to find resource materials on. If you are an avid reader of the daily papers you may find that you already know a lot about the topics, but some good research from trustworthy newspapers and magazine websites will help. BBC, The Guardian, Outlook, The Economist are some good sources of information. Please note, however, that these sources will only give you background information about the topics. These sources may be biased, and hence cannot be the your only reference. In the duration of each session, only Reuters' sources will be permitted. Every committee has a resources section where you can find the respective study guides.

9) Am I supposed to prepare a resolution before the conference commences, or should I prepare one during the conference?

While having a resolution prepared beforehand is an advantage, it should be noted that you might have to change some of your points during committee due to the stands of the other countries. Debates are a very dynamic event, and as such, making a resolution during committee is more beneficial than preparing one beforehand. Having a template for your resolution, however, is recommended.
